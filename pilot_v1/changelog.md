### Achievements & Key Results
#### Phase 1: Empirical Discrimination (v3 Empirical vs. v3)
*   Implemented empirical scoring rules for Criterion 10 (spread of model scores: Level 2 for >= 0.3, Level 1 for >= 0.1, Level 0 otherwise).
*   Ran analysis comparing static `v3` vs empirical `v3_empirical` results using `analyze.py`, showing:
    *   **Top-Mid Gap:** Improved from -0.10 (reversed signal) to +0.28 (correct signal).
    *   **Criterion 10 Variance:** Increased from 0.138 to 0.923 (high discriminative power).
    *   **Judge Noise:** Decreased by 6% (from 0.474 to 0.444).

#### Phase 2: Rubric v4 Rollout & Assertion Quality Refinements
*   Created and rolled out the **v4 Rubric** incorporating **Empirical Discrimination** (Criterion 10) and the new **Contamination Resistance** (Criterion 11).
*   **Assertion Quality Refinement (Criterion 1):** Updated Criterion 1 (Answer Correctness) definitions in `total_rubric_v4.md` to introduce concrete guidelines targeting assertion quality:
    *   *Level 0* now penalizes trivial matching (e.g. `assert output != ""`) and variable mismatches.
    *   *Level 1* penalizes exact matching on free text and generic regexes.
    *   *Level 2* rewards normalization safeguards (whitespace stripping, case normalizing) and anchored example pairs for LLM judges.
*   Adjusted category weights to support 11 criteria (Category A: 5 criteria, Category B: 2 criteria, Category C: 4 criteria; Category C scoring is 0.375 per level, total max score remains 10.0 points).
*   Ran analysis on the v4 results, showing:
    *   **Spearman Correlation (Rho):** Rose to **0.5105** (over the 0.50 threshold, establishing a strong correlation with human calibrations).
    *   **Top-Mid Gap:** Maintained a positive signal at **+0.19**.
    *   **Mid-Bottom Gap:** Widened to **+2.66**, showing stronger segregation of poor benchmarks.
    *   **Contamination Variance:** Stood at **0.633**, indicating high discriminative power for the new criterion.
    *   **Judge Noise:** Decreased to **0.425** (a total **10.3%** noise reduction compared to the static v3 rubric).

#### Phase 3: Two-Tier Scoring Implementation
*   Implemented a **Two-Tier Scoring** pipeline separating **Pre-Run (Static)** and **Post-Run (Empirical)** evaluations to support immediate feedback at upload followed by empirical verification.
*   Ran comparative analysis using `analyze_two_tier.py` to evaluate the value of the empirical post-run phase, showing:
    *   **Spearman Correlation (Rho):** Rose from **0.5022** (Pre-Run) to **0.5105** (Post-Run).
    *   **Top-Mid Gap Correction:** Pre-Run scoring resulted in a negative Top-Mid Gap of **-0.02** (unable to separate top-tier from mid-tier). Post-Run scoring corrected this to **+0.19**, proving that post-run empirical execution is necessary to properly isolate top-tier benchmarks.
    *   **Mid-Bottom Gap:** Widely separated in both phases at **+2.80** (Pre-Run) and **+2.66** (Post-Run).
    *   **Judge Noise:** Fell from **0.4435** (Pre-Run) to **0.4255** (Post-Run).

### Proposals & Suggestions (for Rubric v4)
1.  **Contamination Resistance (New Criterion):**
    *   *Proposal:* Add an 11th criterion to check if benchmark prompts/items exist in model training corpora.
    *   *Rationale:* Evals are ineffective if models are just memorizing.
2.  **Empirical Discrimination (Dynamic Scoring):**
    *   *Proposal:* Move from static model coverage check to dynamic scoring based on actual post-run model score variance.
    *   *Rationale:* Tasks where all models score 100% or 0% fail to discriminate.
3.  **Assertion Quality Anti-Patterns:**
    *   *Proposal:* Add specific Level 0/1 definitions to penalize fragile regex, trivial success criteria (e.g., matching empty strings), and variable template mismatches.
    *   *Rationale:* Tightens validation code robustness.
4.  **Reliability & LLM Judge Determinism:**
    *   *Proposal:* Reward/require determinism checks (temperature=0.0, seeds, self-consistency voting) for tasks using LLM-as-a-judge.
    *   *Rationale:* Minimizes score variance across runs.
5.  **Two-Tier Scoring (Pre-Run vs. Post-Run):**
    *   *Proposal:* Split the rubric into static pre-run scoring (construction/docs) and empirical post-run scoring (discrimination/reliability).
    *   *Rationale:* Provides immediate feedback on upload, followed by complete evaluation after runs.
